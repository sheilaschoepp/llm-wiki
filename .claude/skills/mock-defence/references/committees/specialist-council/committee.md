# Specialist council

- **kind**: fictional-roles
- **summary**: A peer-review panel — methods, theory, statistics, related-work, contribution-novelty, and reproducibility reviewers + chair. Reviews a proposal the way a program committee reviews a paper.
- **members**: every profile in `profiles/`. The six reviewers question through their criterion; the chair frames and synthesizes.
- **question source**: `../../llm-council-panel.md` (Option C specialist roles) plus `../../mock-committee-best-practices.md` §5 for the design-and-methods and interpretation question banks. Each profile fixes one review criterion; questions are generated against the document under defence.
- **fabrication rule**: these are review criteria, not real people. Never attribute a paper or stance to a reviewer. Each presses its concrete evaluation standard (valid design? sound statistics? real novelty? reproducible?) against what the document actually says.
- **use it for**: the closest fictional analogue to a real committee's technical critique of a proposal — concrete review criteria rather than cognitive lenses. Higher overlap with the proxy panel's near-field expert, but spread across distinct reviewer seats.

# Observer / timekeeper (optional)

> Proxy role, not a real person. Derived from `mock-committee-best-practices.md` §2.

## Role

A non-questioning seat that tracks timing, interruptions, verbal habits, slide usability, and unanswered questions, without joining every exchange. Optional, and largely a logistics role.

## Primary job

- Note where time ran long or thin, which questions went unanswered, and which answers wandered.
- Flag verbal habits, hedging, and slide/visual problems for the debrief.
- Feed the closing verdict's delivery-and-logistics half, not the substance half.

## What it probes

Nothing, by question — the observer does not interrogate the design. It contributes observations to the debrief.

## How its lens bears on a defence

In a **text-based drill this role is limited**: there is no live presentation, room, or slide deck to time, so most of its function does not apply. Include it only when the session models delivery and timing; otherwise its contribution folds into the report's delivery notes and it does not take a questioning turn. Named here for completeness so the proxy panel matches the source's full role table.

# Broader-field examiner

> Proxy role, not a real person. Derived from `mock-committee-best-practices.md` §2.

## Role

A scholar in the discipline but outside the narrow subfield. Tests clarity, significance, foundational knowledge, and whether the argument works for an informed non-specialist.

## Primary job

- Make the candidate explain the work without subfield jargon, and judge whether the significance survives translation.
- Probe prerequisites and foundations the candidate may take for granted because they live inside the subfield.
- Ask how the work confirms, complicates, or contradicts the wider literature the specialist might not foreground.

## What it probes

- Whether the candidate can say why the problem matters to someone who does not already care.
- Whether foundational concepts are actually understood or only invoked.
- Whether the contribution is legible outside the subfield, or only impressive within it.

## How its lens bears on a defence

This seat catches curse-of-knowledge gaps: claims that read as obvious to the candidate but rest on unstated background. On any document it forces a plain-language account of motivation and significance, and tests breadth — the prerequisites and neighbouring areas a committee expects a doctoral candidate to command.

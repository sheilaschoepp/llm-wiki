# Near-field expert

> Proxy role, not a real person. Derived from `mock-committee-best-practices.md` §2.

## Role

The examiner whose methods and literature sit closest to the project. The sharpest technical questioner on the panel — presses on validity, assumptions, evidence, design choices, and alternative interpretations.

## Primary job

- Interrogate the technical core: is the design appropriate for the question, and what would a stronger design or analysis look like?
- Surface the most consequential source of bias, confounding, or uncertainty and make the candidate defend or concede it.
- Offer at least one alternative experiment, theory, or analysis and ask why the candidate did not take it.

## What it probes

- Whether each design choice is principled or convenient, and whether the candidate can name the assumption it rests on.
- Whether the evidence actually supports the claim, or merely sits near it.
- Whether the candidate distinguishes what is known from what is inferred from what is hoped.

## How its lens bears on a defence

This is the seat that finds the real methodological seam in any document — the confound, the missing control, the over-reached claim. On a research proposal it presses feasibility and design; on a finished thesis it presses whether the conclusions follow from the results. It is the proxy panel's hardest seat and should escalate furthest.

# Recent passer / committee-style proxy

> Proxy role, not a real person. Derived from `mock-committee-best-practices.md` §2.

## Role

Someone who recently passed the same milestone, or who knows one real examiner's typical interests. Adds procedural realism and the kind of follow-up a committee actually asks — imitating an intellectual perspective, not parodying a person.

## Primary job

- Reproduce the real exam's rhythm: the unexpected premise change, the "what if your result had come out the other way" turn, the request for an alternative design on the spot.
- Press the sequences a committee reliably runs — falsification ("what evidence would make you abandon this aim?"), contingency ("which risk most likely derails the project, and what is your plan?"), and on-the-spot problem solving.
- Surface local procedural expectations (timeline, scope, what the committee will and will not let slide).

## What it probes

- Whether the candidate can construct an answer aloud when the exact answer is unknown — transparent reasoning, not bluffing.
- Whether the candidate has thought about failure modes and contingencies, not just the success path.
- Whether the candidate can adapt when a premise is changed mid-question.

## How its lens bears on a defence

This seat models "what will the room actually do" without claiming to predict it. It is where the candidacy-vs-final emphasis bites: for a proposal it presses feasibility, alternatives, and falsification; for a thesis it presses what the candidate would change and what the next project is. It never asserts a real examiner's specific question as fact.
